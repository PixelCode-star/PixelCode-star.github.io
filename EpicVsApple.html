Epic Sues Apple for Anti-Competitive Behavior


The Situation at Large




Recently Epic Games has added to the long list of people who have recieved the ban hammer from Apple
policy on all income generated from in app purchases on the iPhone. Epic Games being widely
known among their so called fans for creations such as the Fortnight game, which as of August 30,
2020 is the world’s largest battle Royal game and game in general. Epic Games has an in-app

purchase through an in-game currency called V bucks. For a while the only way to attain V-bucks
was through the App-store but Epic decided that 30% of every purchase of V-bucks was way too
high a price and decided to bypass this by introducing a way for people to directly purchase
V-bucks. This is when the battle started with Apple. Apple not liking that a big chunk of its
revenue was cut off went into all out attack mod onto Epic games. Apple then went so far as to
delete Epic Games’ developer account from the App-store.

THE INEVITABLE BAN




Epic Games having directly violated Apples’ terms and conditions of service were or must have been
aware that the ban was inevitable. Apple is not the only app store to ban Epic Games from their store
,Google play too banned Epic Games just last year but that didn’t hurt the company so much as the
Apple ban. Why , you may be wondering. Doesn’t Android run on most devices than IOS devices.
You would be right to say this But There are so many app-stores on Android , people could always just
t download Fortnight on those other stores but for iPhone Phones , the App-store is the only
alternative to acquire apps other than from third party websites which themselves are not very
much encouraged because they could possibly damage your very much expensive iPhone.
The App-store is where Apple can Practise its monopolistic behavior without anyone saying a thing
, or so we all thought.Epic however had a different idea on the entire situation filing a 62 page
lawsuit to Apple for Anti-competitive and Monopolistic behavior. This itself should cast an eye of
suspicion onto Epic, that they knew ,they’d get banned. A good old tale of the Cat provoking the dog.

Why Strike Now




Why does Epic Strike Now. Why would Epic ,a small gaming company compared to the big tech giant that is
Apple pick such a fight. Well its quite simple. Epic chose this to be the best moment to strike
Apple as this is the time that Apple seems to be most vulnerable. Yes if there was a better time to
strike a big tech company, it would be now where these companies are worried about Anti-trust
issues that came up last year. Starting with whole situation between Spotify and Apple music,
Spotify accusing Apple of monopolistic behavior of undercutting prices. Apple is taking hits from
everybody, Microsoft with the X cloud issue competing with the Iphone’s Arcade. Apple seems to
be most vulnerable. These Companies on their own have no single shred of a chance of stopping
apple or infultriating the IOS ecosystem. Apple being a company that is focused on the end all
User experience and control , Epic poses at this time a serious threat. Apple also being
investigated by the EU for anti-competitive behaviour , Apple could not be more vulnerable. If
Epic wins the lawsuit , Developers would be entitled to the same deal but this doesn't seem

appealing to Apple ,giving up almost all their revenue in App Purchase. If Epic however loses ,
This would go extremely bad for epic . The aftermath of this if Epic doesn't win is as I can only say
“Epic Disaster.”. Making enemies with one world’s largest big tech companies is a stupid decision
that shouldn't even be in any CEO’s realm of thought.

THE UNREAL ENGINE





The Unreal engine being widely used by game developers is an Asset of Epic Games. Closing the
unreal engine would be direct and a death blow to epic games but luckily for epic games the
judge ruled that Apple cannot close the Unreal engine as it would impact other developers who
did nothing to Apple. A close call for Epic. Small game developers live to see another day but if I
was a game developer I would think of another alternative because given Apple’s history , this
could go in all directions for Epic.

Epic Games’ Master Plan




Simply Epic must have seen this coming.You don’t bite the dog’s bone and expect it to lie down. It will
come for you with everything its got. Such as I used the example of a dog’s bone. You should never
touch the dog’s bone without having the muscle to back it up. Of-course its not that Epic is starting
a fight for the sake of starting a fight. If it was a company like Google or Facebook ,it would be
understandable and at least probable but for Epic ,it doesn't make much sense.Fortnight was on IOS
first. Apple made Fortnight to what it is today. A dog biting the hand that fed it. Apple is not taking
this lightly. The real reason is that Epic is pushing for their own App-store called Epic Apps and this is
already in existence but with only Fortnight. Apple knows this and we will have to wait to see
how this all unfolds
